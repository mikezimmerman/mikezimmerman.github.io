My client wanted a "flashy" eLearning course that would keep the retail-salesforce target learner audience engaged, so I taught myself Flash over the weekend, then designed and developed 100% all aspects of this course entirely myself, from scratch, start to finish, in two weeks.
The way the credit-card buttons "bounce" in and out? Entirely hand-drawn and hand-animated (one frame at a time) by me, from scratch (no "libraries", no "widgets", no nothing).
The entire UI design and behaviors—including the course title, navigational/menu interface along the top, button design and programming, font selection, text coloration, etc.? Entirely hand-designed and built by me.
All aspects of the graphic design and animation of the "Pop Quiz" UI (first instance of which appears on page 4 of 48 of the "Big Card" course)? Entirely hand-drawn, hand-animated, and color-theoried by me!
The free clipart used to illustrate many of the pages? Alas, a necessary evil imposed by the astonishingly aggressive two-week zero-to-live-in-prod project timeline.
